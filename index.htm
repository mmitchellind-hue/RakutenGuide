🤓 First, the basics

In this email, I talk about 3 US programs - Rakuten, Bilt & Rove. I'm giving a brief intro to all 3 below

  • Rakuten is a program that gives cashback on online shopping done in the US. They give this cashback 4 times in a year - 15th Feb, 15th May, 15th Aug & 15th Nov.
  • Bilt is essentially an online app for paying rents. But they have a tie up with Rakuten, and you can convert their cashback to 17+ hotel/ flight partners, mostly all of them in 1:1 ratio (Accor is 3:2).
  • Rove is the most useful in the long term, especially for those outside the US. It gives points convertible to 15+ hotel/ flight loyalty programs, against hotel bookings, flights, & online shopping.

Something to note. For Bilt & Rove, I am not talking about just the usual hotel & flight partners. Bilt & Rove include some partners that no credit card in India transfers to ... such as Hyatt, Alaska Airlines, etc. But then, there is always the crowd favourite - Accor!!

📐The Points Maths is like this

  • If Rakuten transfers the cashback to Bilt by 15th May, Bilt will give 1000 Bilt points per $10.
  • If the cashback is received after 15th May (highly likely), it would be 500 Bilt points per $10.
  • Bilt points can be transferred in 1:1 ratio to most partners, and 3:2 to Accor.
  • So if you get cashback for 97% of $112 + $50 bonus before 15th May, you get ~15,800 Bilt points. After 15th May, this reduces to ~7,900. So you get ₹21k worth of Accor points before 15th May, ₹10.5k worth of points after 15th May.
  • Bilt comes out with Rent Day Bonuses on the 1st of every month, where they give you bonus points for transfer to one or more of the partners. This really increases the value of your points, and it is worth waiting for these rent day Bonuses.
  • There is still a small chance that you could get it before 15th May, as some of the people from last time have got their cashback in 10 - 12 days.
